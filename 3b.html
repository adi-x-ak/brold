Editors’ picks for the best kids’ books of 2021

Seira Wilson|November 16, 2021


Our number one pick for children’s books is the delightful and heartwarming novel, The Beatryce Prophecy, by award-winning author Kate DiCamillo, with illustrations by Sophie Blackall. The Beatryce Prophecy is an extraordinary tale of courage and found family. This book has all the hallmarks of an instant classic: a beautifully layered story with unforgettable characters who take root in your heart.

Besides our overall top 20 picks for kids, we also chose the top 20 children’s books for each age group, you’ll find a teaser for these below (ages sorted youngest to oldest).


Happy reading!

1) Woodland Dance:

by Sandra Boynton


No.1 for Baby to Age 2

Sandra Boynton is already a favorite for this age group, and her newest board book about a host of cute animals gathering in the forest to play music and have a big dance before they all go to bed is sweet, silly, and perfect for sharing. Babies and toddlers will love the expressive animals, the musical sounds, and the rhyming text.

2) Room for Everyone

by Naaz Khan


No.1 for Ages 3 to 5

Room for Everyone is truly a delight. The book is set in Zanzibar and follows a bus (called a daladala) as it picks up more and more passengers. First there is one old man and his bike with no seat, who really must get out of the heat. Three vendors with baskets of fruit all pile in, and so on. The text has a sing song rhyming rhythm as the story counts up to 10 with new passengers and down again when the bus reaches its destination—the beach. This is a picture book that will make kids and adults smile with every page.

3) Stuntboy, in the Meantime

by Jason Reynolds, drawings by Raúl the Third


No.1 for Ages 6-8


There’s a new superhero in town and his name is Stuntboy. When he’s not being Stuntboy, Portico Reeves is a regular boy who lives in a castle with many doors and windows, otherwise known as an apartment building. He has a best friend named Zola, a nemesis who is the school bully, and anxiety he calls the “frets” that is ramping up as the conflict between his parents gets increasingly worse. Stuntboy, in the Meantime (November 30) is an illustrated story of friendship, family, and growing up; full of crazy stunts, funny sidebars, and colorful cartoon panels. This is the first book of a new series that I think kids are going to love.

View details >
4) The Beatryce Prophecy

by Kate DiCamillo, illustrated by Sophie Blackall


No.1 for ages 9-12 and No.1 best children’s book of 2021

Also available as a beautiful gift edition for the holidays, The Beatryce Prophecy is a classic in the making. DiCamillo’s story tells of a girl who is found near a monastery, holding the ear of a very protective goat and remembering only her name—Beatryce. The monk who finds her soon learns that she also has a dangerous secret, one that reminds him of a prophecy that would unseat the current king. Beatryce sets off on a journey, accompanied by a boy who is brave and kind, and they are joined by two others who become family to her. The characters in this story will stay with you, as will the story itself, just as all good and lasting tales do. This is a book destined to become a favorite for years to come.
