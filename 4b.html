Drew Barrymore's favorite cookbooks

Vannessa Cronin|November 12, 2021


Our picks for the Best Science Fiction and Fantasy of the month is basically a round up of some of the most wildly, gloriously, cleverly imaginative stories, from a retelling of a Greek myth, to a postapocalyptic bodycon tale, to a poignant quest to save the Library of the Unwritten in Hell.

Happy reading!

1) Eat Better, Feel Better: My Recipes for Wellness and Healing, Inside and Out

by Giada De Laurentiis


Giada is a food goddess. I really love the whole ethos behind this book; I dog-eared so many pages! These are wellness recipes that are so amazing because they’re very good for gut flora and wellness and health, but the recipes are so good, and you can actually stick with this way of eating.

View details >
2) Vegetables Unleashed

by José Andrés


José Andrés has the biggest heart ever. His organization, World Central Kitchen, has served more than 40 million fresh meals to people around the world impacted by natural disasters and other crises. And I love his books, too!

View details >
3) Living Lively: 80 Plant-Based Recipes to Activate Your Power and Feed Your Potential

by Haile Thomas


I love food, but I’m not a professional chef, and so I love talking to chefs to get inspired! I was really drawn to the plant-based recipes here, because they’re genuinely how I eat. They might seem a little intimidating at first, but this book proves that plant-based cooking can be a creative and joyful process.

View details >
4) Essentials of Classic Italian Cooking

by Marcella Hazan


I love Italian food, and pasta, so much! As my friend and co-author Pilar Valdes taught me, Marcella Hazen is a tremendous pioneer who taught people to appreciate Italian food in a whole new way. She taught people to recognize and appreciate their own palate, and learn and taste as they go.
